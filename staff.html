Management Team

Dr. Samuel Dotse

Dr. Samuel Dotse (PhD) is a globally recognized leader in environmental governance, sustainable development, and climate diplomacy. As the Founder and Chief Executive Officer of the HATOF Foundation, he has consistently driven initiatives that address critical environmental challenges and foster sustainable growth worldwide. His exceptional expertise and dedication have earned him numerous high-profile roles across national, regional, and global platforms. Dr. Dotse serves as the International Society of Diplomats’ African Special Envoy on Climate Change and Country Coordinator for the Climate Action Network-Ghana. He is a distinguished Technical Advisory Committee member for the Green Climate Fund under Ghana’s Ministry of Finance and a Board Member of the Stakeholder Advisory Network on Climate Finance in the United States.

His contributions extend to the African Union Scientific, Research, and Innovation Council, the African Research and Innovation Partnership, and as the Convener for the Global Environment Facility CSO Network in Ghana. Dr. Dotse’s vast experience includes serving as a Transitional Committee Member of the Forest Investment Program-Dedicated Grant Mechanism for Indigenous People and Local Communities (2010–2017) and as the Regional Focal Point of the Global Environment Facility Civil Society Network for Central and West Africa (2011–2015). From 2011 to 2015, he was the Elected Focal Point of the African Union CSOs and European Union Partnership on Climate Change and Environment. He led the African Union ECOSOCC Delegation to the Africa Global Warming and Development event in Paris in 2015 and was Ghana’s National Coordinator for the Partnership for Action on Green Economy (2014–2016), convening the collective efforts of five UN agencies (UNIDO, UNDP, ILO, UNITAR, and UN Environment). As a highly respected figure in sustainable development, Dr. Dotse also served as Deputy Presiding Officer of the African Union Economic, Cultural and Social Council (ECOSOCC) from 2014 to 2016 and chaired its Rural Economy and Agricultural Cluster from 2011 to 2012. With over 19 years of experience, Dr. Dotse has become a senior expert in environmental governance, advocating sound policies and raising awareness about sustainable development at every level. He works closely with policymakers to review and design effective frameworks for climate finance, helping to address pressing environmental challenges. His extensive experience in International Law and Treaty Law has enabled him to align institutional objectives with national, regional, and international obligations while fostering partnerships among governments, diplomatic entities, and civil society organizations (CSOs).

Ms. MaryJane Enchill

Maryjane Enchill is a seasoned environmental and climate policy expert, currently serving as the Deputy CEO of the HATOF Foundation. She is also a Director and Board Member of the Global Youth Climate Action Fund and the Coordinator of the Climate Action Network-Ghana. With extensive experience in sustainable development, climate finance, and communication, she supports transformational change through capacity building, technical assistance, and policy advocacy. Maryjane has served as a consultant for state institutions, the banking sector, and UN agencies, offering expertise in facilitating and moderating high-level discussions on environmental and sustainability issues. Her work spans national and international platforms, where she speaks, moderates, and serves on juries and panels.

She continues to lead and coordinate youth networks both nationally and globally, advancing youth engagement in environmental governance. As a freelance journalist and environmental project manager, Maryjane also supports grassroots interventions with local communities, women, and youth across Ghana. She contributes actively to global environmental negotiations under the UNFCCC, CBD, UNCCD, and plastic pollution frameworks, bringing a voice of inclusivity and policy relevance to international processes.

MS. BENEDICTA DZAKAH

Benedicta Xorla Dzakah is a dedicated Project and Finance Manager with a strong background in business, finance, and international development. She is known for being proactive and solution-focused, using her skills to help NGOs achieve their goals. Her work mainly supports sustainable development, especially in building climate resilience and driving meaningful change. Benedicta is skilled at aligning financial plans with organizational objectives, managing resources efficiently, and delivering results even under pressure. She is experienced in budgeting, office management, project coordination, and ensuring compliance with donor requirements.

She also has a solid understanding of international standards like ISO 26000 (social responsibility), ISO 14000 (environmental management), and ISO 9001 (quality management). Along with these technical skills, she is emotionally intelligent, engages well with stakeholders, and is passionate about protecting the environment and supporting communities. Recently, Benedicta has developed an interest in carbon accounting and green climate financing. These are key areas for funding environmental projects. She is now contributing to new financing methods that help build climate-resilient communities. Her goal is to become a leading global expert in climate finance.

MR. JOSH E.K DOTSE

Josh Dotse is a renewable energy and sustainability professional with a strong academic and practical foundation. He holds a Master’s degree in Climate Change and Sustainable Development from the University of Ghana and a Bachelor’s degree in Renewable Energy Engineering from the University of Energy and Natural Resources, Sunyani. His expertise spans renewable energy, carbon markets, and climate policy, with a focus on green building initiatives and carbon trading to support Ghana’s NDC targets.

As the Programme & Partnership Officer at HATOF Foundation, Josh leads efforts to build partnerships and supports in coordinating grant activities that promote climate resilience and sustainable development. Josh is also passionate about climate education, advocating for sustainable living and energy efficiency through community outreach and virtual campaigns. His global experience enriches his ability to address climate challenges from a holistic perspective.

SHARON E. DOTSE

Sharon Dotse is a passionate environmental sustainability advocate with over eight years of experience in environmental education, governance, and climate projects. She has contributed to HATOF Foundation’s efforts in national policy development, capacity-building, and climate initiatives.
