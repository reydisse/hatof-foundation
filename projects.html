GCF CSO Readiness Project, Ghana

Capacity Building and Knowledge Management on Climate Change for Civil Society Organizations (CSOs) in Ghana towards the implementation of the Nationally Determined Contributions.

Funder

Green Climate Fund (GCF)

Timeframe

May 2022 – May 2025

Location

Ghana

Beneficiaries

Direct: 40 Ghanaian CSOs
Indirect: Over 200 CSOs in Ghana

Context

For the past 15 years, the government of Ghana has received support from the Global Environment Facility (GEF), The Adaptation Fund, and other multilateral and bilateral agencies, as well as non-state actors, to implement several capacity-building and climate change coordination and policy development initiatives.

Key Gaps Identified:

  • The absence of CSO-tailored projects/programmes that ensures technical capacity development for CSOs
  • Low level of awareness of climate hazards and knowledge about how to manage them
  • Lack of a long-term strategic and action plan for and by CSOs
  • Inadequate technical capacity to develop and effectively implement climate-related projects
  • Inconsistent and inadequate participation of CSOs in national climate change decision-making processes
  • Lack of coordination mechanisms and clear consultative process among CSOs and NGOs

Project Objectives

To strengthen the capacities of 10 Ghanaian CSO networks to contribute efficiently to the Nationally Determined Contributions planning and implementation processes by:

  • Engaging the National Designated Authority, Direct Access Entities, and GCF on project design, selection, budget preparation, implementation, monitoring and evaluation
  • Developing a CSO-tailored training module on preparing funding proposals
  • Developing a five-year Civil Society Climate Change Agenda and Action Plan
  • Developing GCF Concept note proposing how this initiative could be up-scaled nationally

Project Activities

Project Launch and Inception Workshop

Successfully launched the project with stakeholders and conducted inception workshop

Stakeholder Mapping and Need Assessment

Mapped and identified relevant stakeholders, assessed their capacity needs

Capacity Building Workshops

Organized training sessions on climate change project development and implementation

Project Impact

  • Over 100 CSOs in Ghana engaged in the Rio Convention implementation
  • Established the Ghana Environmental CSO Coordination Mechanism (EnVOG)
  • 40 CSOs trained on climate change project development
  • Developed a five-year strategic Civil Society Climate Change Agenda
